\subsection{Interpretability and Model Analysis}
\label{sec:interpretability}

We complement \S\ref{sec:results_optuna}'s inner-validation Optuna
results with held-out \emph{outer-test} QWK for a curated subset of
configurations (Table~\ref{tab:interpretability_outer_test}). Dense
rows aggregate three training seeds to absorb single-retrain variance
($\sigma \lesssim 0.03$ for ChebyKAN, $\lesssim 0.01$ for FourierKAN).
Each 20-feature row is independently tuned by a 50-trial Optuna sweep
on the model's own top-20 features --- selected by native coefficient
importance for the KANs and by mean-$|\mathrm{SHAP}|$ for XGBoost ---
over a search space that covers optimisation, architecture, and the
relevant regulariser ($\lambda$ for KANs, $\gamma$ and leaf
regularisation for XGBoost). XGBoost throughout is XGBRegressor with
QWK-threshold-calibrated outputs. Every KAN configuration in the
table admits exact per-edge symbolic recovery from its basis
coefficients (R$^2 = 1.000$ by construction via the basis-native
extractor); the interpretability distinctions among rows therefore
turn on \emph{how many} edges remain after pruning and whether the
composed model can be written as a single expression.

\subsubsection{Sparse regime: a compact closed-form ChebyKAN}
\label{sec:interpretability_sparse}

At the 20-feature budget, XGBoost (0.611), tuned FourierKAN (0.608)
and our sparse ChebyKAN hero (0.592) are statistically tied --- all
three 95\% bootstrap CIs overlap. The hero is derived end-to-end
from the Optuna-tuned ChebyKAN ([64,64], degree 7): we (i) train
that configuration on all 140 features with L1 sparsity
regularisation at $\lambda{=}0.1$, (ii) take the top-20 features
ranked by that same model's coefficient importance, (iii) retrain on
the restricted subset with LayerNorm removed, and (iv) L1-prune
edges whose mean activation magnitude falls below $2\times 10^{-3}$.
The result keeps 806 of the 5{,}376 KAN-layer edges
($\approx 85\%$ pruning) at essentially no inner-val QWK cost. At
those 806 active parameters it is $\approx 7\times$ more compact than
the matched-budget tuned FourierKAN (5{,}376 edges) and dramatically
more compact than the 700-tree, 37{,}000-leaf XGBoost --- while
remaining within bootstrap noise of both on outer-test accuracy.

Each active edge admits an exact closed-form recovery: every
layer-0 ChebyKAN edge is a polynomial of degree $\leq 7$ in
$\tanh(x)$ read directly off the learned Chebyshev coefficients
(Table~\ref{tab:closed_forms} lists five representatives). With
LayerNorm removed, composing these edges through both KAN layers
yields a single closed-form expression in the 20 retained inputs.
Any prediction then decomposes into exactly-additive per-feature
contributions via integrated gradients along a chosen reference
path --- no post-hoc surrogate is needed.
Figure~\ref{fig:fig1_interpretability} illustrates this for
applicant 55728: starting from a low-risk reference applicant
(score $-0.18$), the integrated-gradient contributions of the
20 features sum to the applicant's predicted score ($4.58$, class
$5$).

The same closed-form structure also yields exact analytic Greeks:
the partial derivatives $\partial y / \partial x_i$ are obtained by
symbolic chain rule rather than by finite differencing or autograd.
Table~\ref{tab:worked_greek} reports $\partial \text{score} /
\partial \text{BMI}$ for applicant 55728 --- the symbolic value
($-1.7043$) agrees with PyTorch autograd to $2 \times 10^{-6}$ and
with central finite difference (O($\varepsilon^2$) truncation) to
$3 \times 10^{-4}$. This property does not transfer to the other
rows of Table~\ref{tab:interpretability_outer_test}: FourierKAN's
basis is not closed under composition (composing trigonometric
expressions through $\tanh$ yields Bessel-style series, not a finite
expression), and the dense ChebyKAN's LayerNorm breaks the
polynomial structure that makes symbolic differentiation exact.

\subsubsection{Summary}

TabKAN occupies a distinct point on the accuracy--interpretability
frontier in this study. At the 20-feature budget the sparse ChebyKAN
hero matches XGBoost and tuned FourierKAN on outer-test accuracy at
more than $10\times$ fewer active parameters, and it is the only
row of Table~\ref{tab:interpretability_outer_test} that further
admits an end-to-end closed-form composed expression and exact
analytic Greeks --- a level of model-native transparency that SHAP
explanations of the alternatives can only approximate.
