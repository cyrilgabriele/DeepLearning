\subsection{Interpretability and Model Analysis}
\label{sec:interpretability}

While Table~\ref{tab:single_objective_optuna} reports Optuna-selected
inner-validation QWK for each architecture, here we evaluate a subset
of configurations on held-out \emph{outer-test} QWK using fixed
ordinal thresholds calibrated on inner validation
(Table~\ref{tab:interpretability_outer_test}). We switch regimes
because the interpretable sparse variants introduced below are single
retrainings and therefore carry no Optuna-selection bias; reporting
their inner-validation QWK against the sweep-selected dense baselines
would be asymmetric.

All four KAN configurations we analyse (Cheby/Fourier $\times$
dense/sparse) admit exact per-edge symbolic recovery:
each learned univariate edge function can be read directly off its
basis coefficients, yielding R$^2 = 1.000$ by construction (the
basis-native extractor does not fit, it reads). The interpretability
distinction between operating points is therefore not \emph{whether}
edges are recoverable but \emph{how many} edges remain and whether
the composed model can be written as a single expression.

\subsubsection{Sparse regime: compact closed-form TabKAN}
\label{sec:interpretability_sparse}

We derive a compact interpretable variant of each KAN flavour by
(i) selecting the 20 highest-importance input features from a
sparsity-regularised baseline, (ii) retraining on that subset with
LayerNorm removed, and (iii) L1-pruning edges whose mean activation
magnitude falls below a threshold tightened until inner-val QWK loss
stays within 0.005. ChebyKAN tolerates 94\% within-architecture
pruning; FourierKAN reaches 79\% under the same QWK-loss budget.
On outer test the sparse variants match or come close to their dense
counterparts (Cheby: 0.607 $\to$ 0.533, $\Delta = -0.074$;
Fourier: 0.592 $\to$ 0.562, $\Delta = -0.030$), so the accuracy cost
of interpretability in this regime is moderate.

Every surviving edge in both sparse variants admits exact closed-form
recovery from its basis coefficients: each ChebyKAN edge is a
polynomial of degree $\leq 6$ in $\tanh(x)$, each FourierKAN edge is
a linear combination of eight harmonic pairs in
$k\pi(\tanh(x)+1)$. Figure~\ref{fig:fig1_interpretability} shows the
learned edge activations for BMI and Wt under both flavours with the
closed-form overlay; the sampled-activation points (grey) lie on the
recovered curve (coloured) exactly.
Table~\ref{tab:closed_forms} exhibits five representative sparse
ChebyKAN layer-0 edges after rounding and dropping small terms.

Because the sparse no-LayerNorm ChebyKAN composes into a single
closed-form polynomial in the 20 retained inputs (Chebyshev
polynomials are closed under composition), it also admits \emph{exact
analytic partial derivatives} --- actuarial ``Greeks'' computed by
symbolic differentiation rather than finite-differencing. For a
representative applicant (row 55728) we computed $\partial y /
\partial \text{BMI}$ three ways
(Table~\ref{tab:worked_greek}); the symbolic value agrees with
PyTorch autograd to $3 \times 10^{-6}$ and with central finite
difference (O($\varepsilon^2$) truncation) to $5 \times 10^{-4}$.
This property does not transfer to the other rows of
Table~\ref{tab:interpretability_outer_test}: FourierKAN's basis is
not closed under composition, and the dense ChebyKAN's LayerNorm
breaks exactness. Analytic-Greek transparency is therefore a
distinguishing feature of the sparse no-LN ChebyKAN configuration.

\subsubsection{Summary}

The same TabKAN family spans both the top-tier accuracy class at the
dense end (Table~\ref{tab:interpretability_outer_test} rows 2--3) and
uniquely transparent structures at the sparse end (rows 4--5). In
particular, the sparse ChebyKAN row combines three properties no
other row achieves simultaneously: 44$\times$ edge sparsity against
the dense baseline (597 vs.\ 26{,}112), a single closed-form
composed expression in the 20 inputs, and exact analytic Greeks.
The accuracy cost of those three properties, against the
best-performing TabKAN configuration in the paper, is 0.074 QWK.
